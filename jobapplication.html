Bard's response:

Here are 3 things you should update on your resume:

  1. Quantify your accomplishments. For example, instead of saying "successfully managed a large portfolio of technology products / programs," say "successfully managed a portfolio of 10+ technology products / programs with a total budget of $50M." This will give potential employers a better understanding of the scope of your work and the impact you have made.
  2. Use keywords throughout your resume. When employers are searching for candidates, they often use applicant tracking systems (ATS) to scan resumes for relevant keywords. Make sure to include keywords from the job descriptions you are applying to throughout your resume, especially in your skills section and work experience descriptions.
  3. Tailor your resume to each job you apply for. Take the time to read carefully through each job description and highlight the skills and experience that are most relevant to the position. You may also want to consider writing a unique cover letter for each job you apply for.

Here are some specific examples of how you can update your resume:

  • Instead of: Successfully managed a portfolio of technology products / programs across multiple industries including Healthcare, Financial Services, Retirement Services, Banking, Retail, Insurance, and Manufacturing.
  • Write: Successfully managed a portfolio of 10+ technology products / programs with a total budget of $50M across multiple industries including Healthcare, Financial Services, Retirement Services, Banking, Retail, Insurance, and Manufacturing.
  • Instead of: Digital transformation roadmap and strategy
  • Write: Led large transformations using deep experience in Digital Technology Transformation, gap analysis, road mapping, implementation, people / process / technology change management partnering with senior executives at CIO level engagement.
  • Instead of: Engineered best in class solutions with business value, quality, security, performance and speed objectives.
  • Write: Architected and delivered multiple cloud-based, scalable, ephemeral architectural solutions - leveraging IaaS and PaaS platforms (GCP, Azure, AWS) that met or exceeded all business value, quality, security, performance, and speed objectives.

I hope this helps!

Based on your experience, here are 3 relevant job titles you should search for:

  • Vice President of Engineering
  • Director of Digital Transformation
  • Enterprise Architect

These job titles are all senior-level positions that require a deep understanding of technology, business, and change management. They also require the ability to lead and motivate large teams.

Here is a brief description of each job title:

  • Vice President of Engineering: A Vice President of Engineering is responsible for the overall engineering strategy and execution of a company. They oversee the development and launch of new products and features, as well as the maintenance and improvement of existing systems. They also work closely with other departments, such as product management and sales, to ensure that the engineering team is aligned with the company's overall business goals.
  • Director of Digital Transformation: A Director of Digital Transformation is responsible for leading the digital transformation of an organization. This includes developing and implementing a digital strategy, as well as managing the transformation of the organization's technology, processes, and culture. Directors of Digital Transformation often have experience in a variety of industries, as they need to be able to understand the unique challenges and opportunities that each industry faces.
  • Enterprise Architect: An Enterprise Architect is responsible for designing and implementing the overall IT architecture of an organization. This includes defining the organization's technology strategy, as well as selecting and implementing the hardware, software, and networks that the organization will use. Enterprise Architects need to have a deep understanding of a wide range of technologies, as well as the ability to see the big picture and understand how all the different pieces of the IT infrastructure fit together.

I hope this helps!
