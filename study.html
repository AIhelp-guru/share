Introduction to Kinematics in one dimension: uniform motion

Kinematics is the branch of physics that describes the motion of objects without considering the forces that cause the motion. Uniform motion in one dimension refers to the motion of an object along a straight line at a constant velocity.

My notes

  • Uniform motion in one dimension is defined as the motion of an object along a straight line at a constant velocity.
  • The kinematic equations that describe uniform motion in one dimension are:
    • x = x₀ + vt (position)
    • v = v₀ (velocity)
    • a = 0 (acceleration)
  • These equations relate the position (x), initial position (x₀), velocity (v), initial velocity (v₀), time (t), and acceleration (a).
  • Practical applications of uniform motion include the motion of a car on a highway, a train on a track, and a ball rolling on a level surface.
